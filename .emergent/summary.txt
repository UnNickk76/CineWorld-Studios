<analysis>**original_problem_statement:**
The user requested a complete overhaul of the game's Player vs. Player (PvP) mechanics, moving away from simple anonymous boycotts to a deep, strategic system based on player-built infrastructures.

**PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS:**
1.  **Infrastructure System:** Create three new upgradeable infrastructures:
    *   **Investigative HQ:** Allows players to investigate boycotts against them, with a chance to identify the attacker. Has daily usage limits based on its level.
    *   **Operations Division:** Allows players to launch boycotts and counter-boycott actions. Has daily usage limits based on its level.
    *   **Legal Department:** Allows players to take legal action against identified attackers. Requires a minimum fame level and Investigative HQ level to unlock.
2.  **PvP Actions & Costs:**
    *   All infrastructure upgrades cost a mix of in-game currency ($) and premium currency (CinePass).
    *   All PvP actions (boycotting, investigating, legal action) cost CinePass.
3.  **New PvP Loop:**
    *   **Boycott:** An Operations action against another player.
    *   **Investigation:** An Investigative action to identify the attacker.
    *   **Counter-Boycott:** A new set of Operations actions. Can be defensive (reduce effects of an incoming attack, even if anonymous) or offensive (a direct counter-attack against an identified player).
    *   **Legal Action:** A Legal action against an identified attacker, with a chance of success based on both players' Legal infrastructure levels, fame, and player level.
        *   **Success:** Causes the target to lose currency, slows their production, and puts them in a temporary PvP cooldown.
        *   **Failure:** The attacker loses currency and takes a small reputation and production penalty.
4.  **UI:**
    *   Create a new, dedicated HQ (Headquarters) page accessible from the main navigation bar to manage and upgrade these new infrastructures.

This follows two major completed tasks in this session:
1.  **Film Production UX Redesign:** Replaced the old tab-based system with a modern, film-centric popup UI.
